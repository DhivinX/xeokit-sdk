Class CameraFlightAnimation

Animates its View's Camera to look at specified objects, boundaries or locations.

Summary
  • Belongs to a View, and is located at cameraFlight
  • Controls the View's Camera , which is located at camera
  • Navigates the Camera to look at a ViewerObject or boundary
  • Navigates the Camera to an explicit position given as eye, look and up vectors
  • Jumps or flies the Camera
  • Smoothly transitions between projections

  • Flies the Camera to a target.

    • When the target is a boundary, the Camera will fly towards the target and stop when the target fills most of the canvas.
    • When the target is an explicit Camera position, given as eye, look and up, then CameraFlightAnimation will interpolate the Camera to that target and stop there.

      } = {}

      Either a parameters object or a Component subtype that has an AABB. Defaults to the Scene, which causes the Camera to fit the Scene in view.

      • Optional aabb?: FloatArrayParam

        World-space axis-aligned bounding box (AABB) target to fly to.

      • Optional duration?: number

        Flight duration in seconds. Overrides duration.

      • Optional eye?: FloatArrayParam

        Position to fly the eye position to.

      • Optional fitFOV?: number

        How much of field-of-view, in degrees, that a target ViewerObject or its AABB should fill the canvas on arrival. Overrides fitFOV.

      • Optional length?: number
      • Optional look?: FloatArrayParam

        Position to fly the look position to.

      • Optional orthoScale?: number

        Animate the Camera's orthographic scale to this target value. See Ortho.scale.

      • Optional poi?: FloatArrayParam
      • Optional projection?: number

        Projection type to transition into as we fly. Can be any of the values of projectionType.

      • Optional up?: FloatArrayParam

        Position to fly the up vector to.

jumpTo

  • jumpTo(params: any): void

  • Jumps the View's Camera to the given target.

    • When the target is a boundary, this CameraFlightAnimation will position the Camera at where the target fills most of the canvas.
    • When the target is an explicit Camera position, given as eye, look and up vectors, then this CameraFlightAnimation will jump the Camera to that target.
